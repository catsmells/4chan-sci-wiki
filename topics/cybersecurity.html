Cybersecurity

 

        Contrary to what the media tells you, the path of information security is not one for beginners. It's as broad as it is difficult, and things you learn one week are likely to be deprecated the next. To maintain an edge over others in the industry requires a life of constant research, and you'll never be perfect. "Hacking" is merely the concept of developing a clever solution for a given problem; "Security Hacking" usually refers to the contents of this page.

 

"Good defense is your problem. Good offense is someone else's."

Prerequisites

While completely subjective, you'll need to know a few things to focus on this path.

- Web Development Basics
- In-Depth Knowledge of Computer Hardware
- Understanding of the Basics of Electrical Engineering
- Understanding of the Concepts of Cryptography
- Proficiency in Networking
- Working Knowledge of Different Operating Systems
- Experience Maintaining at Least One Physical Server
- Proficiency in at Least One Programming Language
- Active in OpSec Practices

Projects
